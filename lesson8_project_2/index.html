Beyoncé Knowles-Carter

How the Queen Bey has brought attention to black culture and became the number one name in music.

She Does It All

In her twenty-some odd years in the industry, Knowles-Carter has achieved movie deals, the most awarded song in history, and recognition from a number of legends. And she's just getting started!

Lifetime Achievement

In 2014, Beyonce was awarded with the Michael Jackson Video Vanguard Award, an honor also given to Janet Jackson, Rihanna and David Bowie.

Give Back. Bey Good

The 'Love on Top' star also finds time to give back to the community, with philanthropies such as the Bey Good Initiative, and her close work with Global Citizens.

Beyoncé: The MJ of Our Generation.

Years ago, no one would ever have dreamed of comparing anyone to the late great Michael Jackson, but Knowles, who has recognized the King of Pop as one of her many inspirations, is easily now apart of that conversation.

Who Run The World? Girls.

While also being a strong vocalist, actress and philanthropist, Beyoncé prides herself on being an intersectional feminist. She never hesistates to remind women, espically women of color, of their worth and that they can do whatever their hearts can dream of. She has especially become a role model to little black girls, as she was the first black women to grace the cover of the September Vogue.

Beychella: Homecoming

Beyoncé's Coachella performance was perhaps one of the most important nights of black pop culture of the 21st century. She gave a voice to hundreds of thousands of black students and alumni of HBCU's, bringing that integral part of black culture to an environment that otherwise would not see it. Beyonce found it important that in today's climate, that she continue to show her hertiage and be proud of it.
